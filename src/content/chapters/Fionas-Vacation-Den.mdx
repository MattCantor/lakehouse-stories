---
title: Fiona’s Vacation Den
---

Fiona checked her packing list. She had everything she needed for three weeks at her family’s vacation den at Sprained Ankle Stream. She tossed her bag over her back, humming to herself as she pushed a rock over to the entrance to her underground room. She rubbed her paws on the dry earth. 

It was the second day of a surprise break at Foresthood School. A heat wave was going to take, most likely, three weeks away from everyone’s work and school. Fiona’s family was going to head to Sprained Ankle Stream for the entire time. It would be the first time Fiona had gone for summer. 

Normally, the second winter began and school ended, Fiona’s family would leave their den and walk along a path in the forest to get to the stream. In spring, summer and fall, it was filled with rushing water, however in winter it was frozen over. They’d follow the stream all the way into a place where there was a tiny shallow pond that the stream went through, and it had big rocks with moss and grass growing on them. Under many of them, there were holes going a little ways under the earth, and they were always damp but still strong and sturdy. Most of these were vacation dens, owned and rented, but a few were real homes people lived in year-round, even when the stream was filled with water. 

One of them was the fox family’s own. It had been dug by a fox unlike most of the others which had been made by moles. It was well-suited to what they wanted because of this. 

Fiona’s bed was made of wet moss, seaweed, and twigs. At her normal den, the bed was made of dirt. She was very proud of it-she had collected, fluffed and laid out the dirt herself, and added daisies sometimes, which usually lasted two weeks. 

However, she loved her vacation bed because of the cool seaweed and fluffy wet moss, and how the twigs around it made it look like a bird’s nest. Since it was summer they would need to wring out and soak the seaweed daily so it didn’t dry out-which sounded kind of fun. She had a wet rock to dab at the moss before bed every night too. But she had important matters to tend to. 

“Can I see Dolly?” She asked her mom, who was restocking their kitchen with chicken at the moment.
