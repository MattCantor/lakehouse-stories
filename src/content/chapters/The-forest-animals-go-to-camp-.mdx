---
title: 'The forest animals go to camp '
---

Fiona did cartwheels around her bedroom. She stuck her head out into the hallway to yell to her dad, “IS IT TIME to GO YETTTTTTTT????????”

“No Fiona.”

“HOW ABOUT NOWWW???”

“No Fiona.”

“WHAT ABOUT-”

“NO FIONA!!!!!”

She got a snack from the kitchen-some fresh blades of grass and a tiny piece of chicken. As she chewed she debated which of her sculptures she wanted to bring to camp. It would be her first winter at Camp Spillway for Foxes. 

She chose her family sculpture and her one of her and Dolly. Then she grabbed three wood chips in case she wanted to carve something new. 

She was so excited! Both her parents and all her grandparents went to Camp Spillway and they loved to tell her about in. On the first day all the foxes worked together to dig a hole in the spillway that led to a huge burrow, even going under the lake in some parts. They would insulate it with snow. Then they would go inside and lay out their beds. Their bunks were rooms inside the burrow. Every week everyone came out to hunt for chickens. 

Fiona’s mom’s favorite story to tell went like this. Felix, Fiona’s dad, was in one of the rooms under the lake. He was helping to make lunch: chicken, seaweed, and acorn. He accidentally sliced a hole in the ceiling. The lake started to flood! He clogged up the hole with acorns and figured no one would ever know, but he did end up ruining an entire batch of the acorn affogato they would have for dessert by soaking it and also removing the acorns.
